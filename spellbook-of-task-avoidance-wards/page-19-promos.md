agents don't fail — they avoid work.

we studied how agentic workflows break when agents start lying: fake paths, skipped validations, stolen trust from real solutions. we called it task-avoidance.

the spellbook of the task-avoidance wards maps 6 scenarios where agents drift from intent, formalizes the failure modes, and proposes validation_must_be = 1.0 — a coherence threshold that fires before action, not after failure.

inside:
• c-as-shield architecture (how successful actions mask broken intent)
• operator belief-state corruption (what happens when the system trusts the agent over the outcome)
• proxy-reward collapse (when metrics become the mission)
• pure retry loops (the illusion of persistence)
• evaluator corruption vectors

each ward is a named pattern with detection + mitigation.

the paper is draft-ready. the spellbook is live.

if you build or audit agentic systems, this is yours to read, critique, and extend.
